The PUP Pasig River serves as a source of inspiration and a reminder of the need for environmental stewardship. It provides opportunities for research, educational activities, and community engagement related to river restoration, water conservation, and environmental sustainability.

APOLINARIO MABINI
SHRINE

The shrine commemorates Mabini's life, contributions, and enduring legacy as a philosopher, lawyer, and statesman during the Philippine Revolution and the early years of the Philippine Republic. Mabini, also known as the "Brains of the Revolution," played a crucial role in shaping the country's struggle for independence and the formation of its government.

The PUP-Apolinario Mabini Shrine showcases artifacts, documents, and memorabilia that highlight Mabini's life and achievements. It offers visitors a glimpse into his personal and professional journey, providing insights into his philosophical ideas, political beliefs, and unwavering commitment to the Filipino people.

The PUP-Apolinario Mabini Shrine is not only a historical landmark but also a testament to the university's dedication to preserving and promoting Philippine heritage and culture. It serves as an inspiration for students and the wider community, encouraging them to uphold Mabini's ideals of nationalism, integrity, and social justice in their own lives.
